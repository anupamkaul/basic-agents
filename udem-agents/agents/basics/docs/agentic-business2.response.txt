### Opportunity Overview

The financial advisory industry is undergoing a significant shift with increasing consumer demand for personalized, low-cost, and accessible investment advice. Agentic AI systems—AI that can autonomously analyze financial data, understand client goals, make portfolio decisions, and adapt to market changes—can transform this space by providing scalable, intelligent advisory services without human intervention.

---

### Total Addressable Market (TAM) Analysis

1. **Market Definition:**
   - *Robo-advisory market,* part of the broader wealth management and financial advisory industry, refers to AI-driven platforms that manage investment portfolios and provide advice.
   - This includes retail investment advisory, wealth management for mass affluent clients, and eventually expansion into institutional advisory.

2. **Market Size Estimates:**

   - **Global Wealth Management Market:**
     - McKinsey estimates global wealth management assets under management (AUM) at around $100 trillion (2023).
     - Digital channels influence a growing portion of this market, estimated to rise substantially.

   - **Robo-Advisory Market Size:**
     - According to a report by Grand View Research (2023), the global robo-advisory market was valued at approximately $1.2 billion in 2022.
     - Expected CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate) is around 25-30% over the next decade.
     - Expected to reach $20 billion+ SaaS and transaction fees revenue by 2030.

3. **TAM Drivers:**

   - Increasing adoption of digital financial services by millennials and Gen Z.
   - Growth in retail investing driven by lower barriers to entry.
   - Demand for low-cost, scalable advisory services post-2008 financial crisis.
   - Rising complexity in financial products demands AI-driven analysis.
   - Regulatory acceptance and frameworks increasingly accommodating robo-advisors.

4. **Agentic AI Enhancement:**

   - Current robo-advisors typically rely on preset algorithms and limited automation.
   - Agentic AI can fully autonomously manage portfolios, dynamically rebalance, and handle complex client interactions.
   - This leap can open access to a much larger segment, including smaller investors and niche markets underserved by existing advisors.
   - Potential to enter adjacent financial services: tax optimization, retirement planning, insurance advising, etc.
